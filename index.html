The Problem

A $26 Billion Market
Running on Trust Alone.

01
No Scalable Verification

Authentication today relies on individual experts, marque specialists, and fragmented club registries. There is no universal, scalable layer that can assess a vehicle's provenance objectively at transaction speed.

02
Identity Fraud Is Rampant

Clone cars, VIN swaps, and mis-stamped chassis are endemic. A convincingly presented Ferrari clone can transact at $2M+ before any discrepancy surfaces — often in a different jurisdiction entirely.

03
Fragmented Documentation

Ownership history is scattered across auction catalogues, rally registration books, private correspondence, and manufacturer archives in multiple countries and languages. No one aggregates it systematically.

The Solution

Authentication
as Infrastructure.

Halvex operates as an intelligence platform — not an appraisal service. We build a structured, computable record of each vehicle's identity: where it was built, every hand it has passed through, every significant event in its life, and what the market should rationally price it at.

Our AI models are trained on auction outcomes, factory build data, condition photographs, and provenance documentation going back to 1945.

"The difference between a $400K car and a $2M car is rarely the metal. It's the paper trail — and its verifiability."

🔎
VIN History Tracing

Deep cross-referencing of chassis, engine, and body numbers against manufacturer archives, club registries, DVLA/DMV databases, and historical auction records across 14 jurisdictions.

📸
Photographic Assessment

Computer vision trained on 200,000+ collector car images evaluates panel gaps, paint originality, casting marks, interior wear patterns, and period-correct components from submitted photography.

📄
Provenance Documentation

Structured aggregation of ownership history, competition entries, restoration invoices, factory correspondence, and press appearances into a single, tamper-evident digital dossier.

📊
Market Valuation

Regression models built on 60,000+ auction results deliver defensible fair-market estimates adjusted for provenance quality, condition grade, market cycle, and comparable sales velocity.

Process

Market

The Infrastructure
a Market of This Scale Demands.

$26B+
Global classic car market

The collector car market transacts over $26 billion annually at auction alone, with an equal volume in private treaty — none of it with a standardised authentication layer.

15–30%
Provenance premium on verified cars

Cars with documented, verifiable provenance consistently realise a 15–30% premium at major auction houses vs. comparable unverified examples.

~40%
Ferrari 250s with disputed provenance

Industry estimates suggest a significant proportion of the most valuable collector cars carry provenance gaps, inconsistencies, or outright misrepresentation.

The market currently relies on a cottage industry of individual marque experts, auction house specialists, and marque clubs. This is slow, expensive, non-scalable, and subject to conflicts of interest.

Halvex is the first attempt to build a systematic, AI-augmented authentication layer that can operate at market scale — applicable across marques, borders, and transaction types.

  • Insurers need defensible valuations for agreed-value policies on high-value collector cars
  • Finance providers need authenticated collateral assessments before extending credit
  • Auction houses need pre-sale authentication to protect their reputations and buyer relationships
  • Private buyers need independent verification uncorrupted by seller interest
  • Estate attorneys need objective appraisals for succession and inheritance purposes
Founder

The Person
Building Halvex.

SR
Saloni Rakholiya
Founder & CEO — Halvex

"The classic car market is a trust economy masquerading as a financial one. We're building the infrastructure to make trust computable."

Saloni Rakholiya founded Halvex after observing first-hand how fragmented and opaque the authentication process was for high-value collector vehicles. With a background spanning automotive history, data engineering, and market intelligence, she identified that the gap wasn't expertise — it was infrastructure.

The insight at the core of Halvex: every fraud in this market is ultimately an information asymmetry problem. The seller knows something the buyer doesn't. Halvex closes that gap systematically — not car by car with human specialists, but at scale with a computable, defensible authentication record for every significant vehicle.
